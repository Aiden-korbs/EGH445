## Connectivity Types
* **Isolated Equilibrium Points**: A specific, countable fixed point where a region can be constructed around it containing no other equilibrium points.
* **Non-Isolated Equilibrium Points**: A continuum of fixed points (like a line, orbit, or plane) where a region around one point contains infinitely many other connected equilibrium points.

## Quantity Types
* **Finite Equilibrium Points**: The number of stationary points in the system is countable.
* **Infinite Equilibrium Points**: The number of stationary points is uncountable, common in systems with neutral stability or specific linear systems lacking full rank.
